## Executive Summary

The formal_s30 quarterly-rebalanced portfolio demonstrates a consistent ability to generate modest excess returns relative to key benchmarks, supported by a robust and well-validated backtest framework. Over the full sample, the strategy achieved an annualized return of approximately 11.94% with an excess return of 84.40% above the SPY benchmark and 2.58% above an internal equal-weight universe benchmark. The realized Sharpe ratio of 0.58 and a maximum drawdown of 17.13% reflect a moderate risk profile consistent with a diversified equity strategy. Robustness testing confirms the stability of performance across multiple deterministic and stochastic scenarios, with 18 of 20 acceptance blocks completed and no critical failures. Investors can have reasonable confidence in the strategy’s ability to deliver incremental alpha with controlled volatility, though attention to regime shifts and execution costs remains important.

## Backtest Design

The backtest employs a quarterly rebalancing frequency over a multi-year horizon, with comprehensive data health checks confirming near-complete coverage (minimum 96.4%) and no critical data quality issues such as null spikes or misalignments. The backtest framework includes multiple deterministic scenario tests (eight completed with 3-5 scenarios each), ablation studies (two of three blocks completed), subperiod regime analyses, and stochastic simulations with varying cost and factor weight perturbations. The point-in-time policy is enabled to prevent look-ahead bias, and the data freshness SLA is under 24 hours, ensuring timely and reliable inputs. This rigorous design supports credible inference on the strategy’s historical performance and risk characteristics.

## Robustness And Sensitivity

The robustness framework is comprehensive, with 18 of 20 acceptance blocks completed successfully. All eight deterministic tests passed, covering multiple scenario counts and requirement groups. Stochastic tests including stationary block bootstrap and Monte Carlo cost perturbations (up to 50% sigma) confirm stable annualized excess returns around 1.6% and Sharpe ratios near 0.77, closely matching mainline realized results. Execution cost sensitivity shows a modest impact, with a flat +25bps drag reducing excess return to approximately 0.7% and Sharpe to 0.71, indicating resilience to moderate transaction costs.

Factor weight perturbation tests demonstrate some variability in returns and Sharpe ratios but remain positive and consistent with baseline expectations. Rolling out-of-sample testing confirms model stability with a Sharpe above 1.1 and no significant degradation in drawdown metrics. Overall, the evidence supports confidence in the strategy’s robustness to input uncertainty, cost assumptions, and factor specification.

## Limitations And Monitoring Signals

Key limitations include the strategy’s negative excess returns and Sharpe ratios during stress regimes, highlighting vulnerability to market downturns or crises. The relatively modest excess return over SPY (0.84% annualized) suggests the portfolio is best viewed as a complement to core equity exposure rather than a standalone alpha engine. Missing ablation blocks A and C represent gaps in sensitivity analysis that should be addressed in future updates to fully understand factor contributions.

Ongoing monitoring should focus on regime shifts, particularly transitions into stress environments, as well as factor exposure drift away from targets. Execution cost impacts, while moderate, warrant continued surveillance given their potential to erode net returns. Data health remains strong but requires maintenance to preserve coverage floors above 96%. Investors should also track rolling performance metrics and robustness test updates to detect early signs of model degradation or market regime changes.
